Founder & CEO

James Banda Jr

Founder & Chief Executive · Chimera Investment Group Ltd

James Banda Jr. founded Chimera Investment Group in 2022 on a single conviction: Zambia's greatest opportunities already exist. The work is not to invent new industries, but to build stronger, more disciplined businesses within the sectors where the country already has a natural advantage — financial services, commercial agriculture, mineral trading, and technology.

Rather than building one company, he chose to build a diversified operating group able to apply the same standards of management, capital discipline, and operational rigour across very different markets. Each Chimera subsidiary was established to address a specific, practical constraint: limited access to affordable finance, agricultural production operating below potential, fragmented and low-trust mineral markets, and the slow adoption of technology across Zambian business.

He remains directly involved in daily operations. His day typically begins with production updates from Chimera Premium Poultry Farms before turning to KulimaAI, Chimera Technologies' first software platform, which he leads personally — directing product design and AI-assisted development, and running the application inside the Group's own poultry operations ahead of commercial release. Subsidiary managers report on a structured weekly cadence, allowing him to focus on systems, strategy, and removing operational obstacles rather than managing day-to-day tasks.

That operating discipline has been tested. Before focusing on poultry, the Group spent nearly two years building a piggery, scaling from roughly twenty piglets to more than twelve hundred pigs. When the 2023–24 drought drove feed costs sharply higher while market prices held flat, James made the difficult decision to sell down almost the entire herd. The experience reshaped how he runs the Group: he now plans for climatic, economic, and political risks in advance, rather than reacting once they become operational crises.

Alongside Chimera, James serves as a Program Specialist with the African Leaders Malaria Alliance (ALMA), supporting African governments in strengthening accountability through digital scorecards and performance monitoring — work that reinforced his belief that durable organisations depend on clear accountability and disciplined execution.

He holds a Bachelor of Business Administration from Business School Lausanne, studied Business Management at Dublin Business School, and specialised in Digital Marketing and Social Media at CREA Genève. Before founding Chimera, he co-founded Project for Advancement and Individual Development (P.A.I.D.).

A husband and father, James believes every business should be built with a long-term view and a reputation worth inheriting. His ambition for Chimera is to help prove that businesses built in Zambia can compete through discipline, trust, and consistent execution.
